Question 4: ( Part 4 )¶

How do you explain the difference in the behavior of training and testing error as a function of K?¶

Answer:¶

As we are increasing the value of K, it has a big effect on the behavior of training and testing error. We can see clearly in the Graphs when we are using the minimum value of K then the training and testing errors are maximum and when we are using maximum value of k then the training and testing errors are minimum.¶

When we use K = 1, training and testing errors are around 90 %¶

When we use K = 10, training error is around 5 % and testing error is around 25 %¶

Use can see huge difference in the behavior of training and testing error with the change of K.¶

Question 4: ( Part 5 )¶

Based on your analysis, what is a good setting of K if you were deploy your decision tree classifier to classify handwritten digits?¶

Answer:¶

Based on our analysis, 10 is a good value of K when we were deploy our decision tree classifier to classify handwritten digits because decision tree classifier is performing best when we use K = 10.¶

Its accuracy is maximum and error is minimum on taining and testing data with K = 10.¶
